  • There must be 2 – 4 players
  • Each player chooses a colour as their “home” (no duplicates)
  • Each player rolls one dice and the player with the highest number goes first, second highest goes second and so forth
  • Players most roll a six on either die to move one of their men outside of the home
  • If a player rolls two 6s, they may either choose to move one man outside and subsequently move that single man six positions forward or move two men out of the house
  • If a player fails to roll a six on their turn and none of their men are outside the house, they lose their turn
  • If a player places two of their men on the same tile, that is called a “block” and all opposing players behind it cannot advance forward
  • If Player A lands on the same tile as Player B’s man, Player B must return that man to the home
  • The goal is to get all men into the safehouse, marked by the triangle at the top
  • For one man to get into the safehouse, each man must traverse the entire board before advancing upward towards the safehouse
  • No other players can enter the vertical path leading up to the safehouse, so each player’s men are in a safe-zone once they reach that landmark and cannot be sent back to the original home
  • Players cannot move their men backwards after making a move
  • Only one person can win and the game ends when all four men of a single player are in the safehouse
